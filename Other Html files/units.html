Islands are host to diverse plant and animal life. Oceanic islands have the sea as a natural barrier to the introduction of new species, causing the species that do reach the island to evolve in isolation.

Humans have lived on and traveled between islands for thousands of years at a minimum. Some islands became host to humans due to a land bridge or a continental island splitting from the mainland.

Islands became the target of colonization by Europeans, resulting in the majority of islands in the Pacific being put under European control.

Snorkeling or Scuba Diving:Explore underwater ecosystems filled with colorful coral reefs, fish, and other marine life. Many islands have rental facilities and guided tours for both activities.
