A descriptive essay is a type of writing that aims to provide a detailed and vivid description of a person, place, object, or event. The goal is to create a clear and sensory-rich experience for the reader, helping them visualize and feel what the writer is describing. Descriptive essays focus on using rich, detailed language to engage the reader’s senses—sight, sound, touch, taste, and smell. The structure typically includes an introduction, body paragraphs that elaborate on specific details, and a conclusion that summarizes the overall impression or meaning.a paragraph can be 300 words if it is well-structured and coherent. Longer paragraphs are often found in academic or technical writing where detailed explanations are necessary. However, for most writing, shorter paragraphs are preferred as they are easier to read and help maintain the reader’s interest and engagement.
